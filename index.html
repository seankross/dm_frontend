Who makes more money?

People with master's and Ph.D. degrees often work in either industry (companies) or in academia (colleges and universities)

Imagine you were presented with the following two charts that show information about the salaries that people earn in different work settings and with different degrees. The charts were generated from a salary survey. The points in each chart show average salaries and the bars represent margins of error (95% confidence intervals) around the averages.

This chart shows that people with master’s degrees make more money on average than people with PhD degrees. It contains survey responses from 2100 people with master’s degrees and 900 people with PhD degrees.
This chart shows that within both academic and industry work settings, people with master's degrees make less on average money on average than people with Ph.D. degrees. It contains survey responses from 2100 people with master’s degrees (1800 who work in industry and 300 who work in academia) and 900 people with PhD degrees (300 who work in industry and 600 who work in academia).


Given the trends in the two charts, is it possible that these two charts are made up of the exact same data, just grouped differently?



It turns out that these two charts are made up of the exact same data, just grouped differently. That is, 3,000 people’s salaries are represented in the left graph and the same 3,000 people’s salaries are represented in the right graph.
